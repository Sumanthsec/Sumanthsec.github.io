The Vineyard

Discover our terroir, vines, and varietals

Terra Rossa Soil

Our terra rossa soil—a vibrant, iron-rich clay—imparts a subtle yet profound influence on the grapes. Renowned in some of the world’s most prestigious wine regions, this soil provides optimal drainage while retaining enough moisture to sustain our vines through warmer periods. Its mineral content, born from ancient geological activity, contributes to the concentration, complexity, and longevity of our wines.

Rolling Hills & Microclimates

The gentle slopes of Pilot Hill define our topography. Their varying aspects offer a mosaic of microclimates—allowing precise placement of varietals to maximize sun exposure and mitigate extremes. Cooling breezes flow across the elevated terrain, preserving the grapes’ acidity and delicate aromatics. This natural elevation ensures a balanced fruit profile, avoiding over-ripeness while capturing depth and nuance.

Matured Vines

Our vineyard blocks house vines that are decades old, with deep roots intimately connected to the terra rossa. These venerable vines yield fewer clusters, but each berry is rich in flavour, with thicker skins and richer tannins. The resulting wines possess an intense color and remarkable structural integrity, enabling them to age gracefully and evolve over time.

Varietals & Winemaking

We cultivate classic Bordeaux varietals: Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, Petit Verdot, and Malbec. Each varietal is carefully tended with precise pruning and canopy management. Hand-harvesting at optimal ripeness and rigorous sorting ensure that only the finest fruit makes it to our cellar. The wines emerging from our crimson slopes reveal dark fruits with hints of cedar, tobacco, and subtle minerality. They are balanced, with silky tannins and a lingering finish that speaks of complexity and refinement.
